The Gabunga — Admiral of the Royal Navy

The Mmamba clan holds the hereditary court title of Gabunga — Admiral of the Kabaka's royal navy on Lake Victoria. Historically the Gabunga commanded royal canoes (Enyanja), protected the King's waterways, and controlled trade across Lake Victoria and the Sese Islands.

Origin Story

The Mmamba clan traces its origin to Kintu, the first Kabaka of Buganda, through the lineage of Nambi. The lungfish — which can survive both in water and on land, enduring long dry seasons buried in earth — became the clan's totem as a symbol of resilience and enduring life.

The Mmamba are historically associated with Lake Victoria's shores and the Sese Islands (Sseze Ssaza), where the lungfish is found in abundance. As the clan of the Gabunga, the Mmamba commanded the Kingdom's most vital waterway.

Sigas (Sub-clan branches)

  • Nkima

    The Monkey siga — central Buganda, historically connected to the royal court.

  • Kasimba

    The Genet Cat siga — Masaka and Rakai districts.

  • Nkerebwe

    The Crocodile siga — warriors and navigators; associated with the Sese Islands and the Gabunga's fleet.

Taboos (Mizizo)

  • A Mmamba may not eat the lungfish (omuziro)
  • A Mmamba may not eat Muguya (kob antelope — akabbiro)
  • A Mmamba may not kill or mistreat a lungfish; if found on land, return it to water
  • A Mmamba may not marry another Mmamba — exogamy is strictly observed

The Bataka — Clan Heads of Buganda

43 Abataka collectively form the Olukiiko lw'Abataka — the Council of Clan Heads advising the Kabaka.

About the Olukiiko lw'Abataka: All 43 Bataka collectively advise the Kabaka on clan governance, land rights, and cultural heritage. The Foundation works in partnership with this council. Clan verification is conducted through the Bataka.
