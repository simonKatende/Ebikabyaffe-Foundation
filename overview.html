Ebikabyaffe Foundation · App Structure Overview

Member Mobilization Web App

"Reclaim who you are — discover your clan, your roots, your people."

Endorsed by Buganda Council of Clan Heads (Olukiiko lw'Abataka) Audiences Baganda in Uganda + Diaspora (52 clans) Spec date 10 May 2026 · Status: Draft for Foundation review
Foundation Goals
GOAL 01

Mobilize members at scale — recruit a credible base of self-identified clan members across all 52 clans for lobbying authority.

GOAL 02

Generate sustainable funding — convert engaged members into recurring Busuulu donors funding the school, Bataka, and operations.

GOAL 03

Strengthen the Bataka — give clan heads a digital channel to verify members, reach their clan, and be publicly visible as authorities.

GOAL 04 · IMPLICIT

Earn Kingdom endorsement — produce a product so dignified and accurate that Mengo wants its name associated with it.

Dual Audiences

🇺🇬 Baganda in Uganda · Ages 20–55

  • Smartphone-using, mobile-money-comfortable
  • Pay in UGX via MTN MoMo / Airtel Money
  • Default language: Luganda
  • Mobile-first, often on 3G — data is cost-sensitive
  • Know their clan; may not know siga or mutuba
  • Give smaller amounts, more frequently
  • Respond to concrete causes (school roof, clan event)

🌍 Diaspora Baganda · UK / US / CA / Gulf / SA

  • Earn in foreign currency (USD, GBP, EUR)
  • Pay by card, Apple Pay, Google Pay, PayPal
  • Default language: English (children rarely speak Luganda)
  • Excellent connectivity
  • Emotionally invested in heritage; disconnected from daily clan life
  • Give larger amounts episodically
  • Respond strongly to identity narratives

Verification Chain — 3 States
Self-Declared

Automatic at sign-up

Member picks a clan. No further action needed. Counts toward total membership.

  • Full content access
  • Can donate
  • Dashboard and clan page
  • Private family tree
Lineage-Provided

Auto-upgraded by system

Member fills siga, mutuba, and father's name. No human approval needed. Realistic cap for diaspora.

  • Everything in Self-Declared
  • Eligible to request Bataka verification
  • Deeper cultural content unlocked
Bataka-Verified ✓

Approved by Omutaka

Omutaka reviews and approves the lineage claim. Gold standard. Auditable — stored permanently.

  • Verified badge on public profile
  • Downloadable Clan ID card
  • Vote in clan polls
  • Connected family trees
  • Heritage contributor status
  • Priority RSVP at events

Technical Shape

Suggested Stack

  • Next.js (web + API routes)
  • PostgreSQL (hosted, PITR enabled)
  • Tailwind CSS
  • Resend or Postmark (email)
  • Web Push (PWA — no native app v1)
  • S3-compatible storage (photos, audio)

Payment Rails

  • Flutterwave — MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, UGX card
  • Stripe — diaspora card, Apple/Google Pay, recurring
  • PayPal — secondary for older diaspora donors
  • ⚠ Stripe needs Uganda fiscal sponsor entity — solve in parallel

Infrastructure

  • CDN with African edge nodes (Cloudflare)
  • SSR/SSG for clan + campaign pages (SEO)
  • Target: <3s FMP on 3G Uganda mobile
  • Bilingual from day one (lg / en)
  • 6 components, independently deployable
Key Risks & Mitigations
R1
Clan content incomplete at launch — empty clan pages destroy the value proposition.
8–12 week pre-launch content sprint. Six "showcase" clans go deep; the rest get minimum-viable pages. Completeness indicator invites member contributions.
R2
Bataka don't engage the admin panel — no verifications, no calls, app loses convening power.
Phased onboarding: start with 6–10 willing Bataka. Foundation posts as proxy for slow adopters. Queues auto-escalate to Foundation after 14 days.
R3
Stripe / fiscal sponsor not solved — diaspora donations are critical revenue.
Solve entity question in parallel with development. If unresolved by month 3, launch with Flutterwave-only; add Stripe as v1.5.
R4
Recurring mobile money is fragile — MTN/Airtel auto-debit reliability is lower than cards; members see "payment failed" and churn.
Expect re-approval in the UX. Send "tap to confirm" nudge 2 days before charge. Treat failures as soft. Offer bank standing order for high-tier sustainers.
R5
Privacy / data protection complaint — Uganda PDPO or GDPR complaint would be painful.
Conservative defaults (no member directory, lineage private). Bilingual privacy policy. Named Data Protection Officer. Maintained data flow document.
R6
Cultural content disputes — contested clan origin stories invite pushback from elders.
Every clan page reviewed and signed off by the relevant Omutaka. Where genuine dispute exists, the page acknowledges it openly.
R7
Buganda Kingdom never endorses — or actively distances from the app.
Design works fully on Bataka endorsement alone. Kingdom endorsement is additive. No Kabaka imagery or royal seals until formally cleared by Mengo.
