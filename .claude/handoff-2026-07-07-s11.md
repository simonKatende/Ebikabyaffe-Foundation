## What was done this session (s11)

Five distinct, independently-requested pieces of work:

1. **Flagship Project expanded into its real three-project structure.**
   `Ebikabyaffe_Foundation_Proposal.pdf` revealed the Foundation runs three
   interconnected projects, not one school: **I. Ekikakyo** (the Clan Fund),
   **II. the Pilot School** (Wakivule Village, Kikyusa Sub-County — corrected
   from a stale "Town Council"), and **III. the Sacco** (Fraternity Sacco,
   real fixed figures: UGX 10,000 membership fee, UGX 20,000/share, any-
   amount donations). Home page's Flagship section (`HomeLanding.tsx`) now
   has three sub-sections (Project I/II/III), each linking to a matching
   campaign on `/give`. `GiveContent.tsx`'s `Campaign` type gained optional
   funding-stats fields — only Wakivule has a real raised/goal to show a
   progress bar; Ekikakyo renders an aim list instead (no invented numbers);
   the Sacco renders its real fee/share/donation figures + a contact note
   instead of a fabricated payment flow. Wired `/give?campaign=<id>`
   deep-linking through the page's `searchParams` (same server-component
   pattern as `/clans?view=bataka`).

2. **"Kiganda" replaced with "Buganda" everywhere** (12 occurrences across
   `AbakunguContent.tsx`, `AbalangiraContent.tsx` ×7, `GiveContent.tsx`,
   `HomeLanding.tsx`, `QueensContent.tsx`, `KabakaContent.tsx`) — a direct,
   unambiguous find-and-replace request.

3. **Kooti ya Kisekwa (the Traditional Court of Clans) added as a third
   `/clans` view** (`?view=kisekwa`), condensed from `Kooti_ya_Kisekwa.pdf`.
   New `KisekwaContent.tsx` covers overview, historical background, the
   judicial escalation hierarchy, jurisdiction, all four landmark rulings
   (Ndiga 2020, Ngeye 2016, Mmamba/Gabunga 2025, Engeye/Kalindaluzzi 2026),
   significance, and quick reference. This surfaced a real, useful
   cross-reference: `bataka.ts` already mentioned "the Kisekwa clan court" by
   name in the Ngeye and Nvubu notes without ever explaining it — this page
   is that missing explainer. Went further and used the PDF to fix live data
   gaps rather than just linking past them: **Ndiga** had no documented
   current head before — the PDF names one (Lwomwa Luggya Bbosa Tabula, via
   the 2020 ruling) — added to `clans.ts` and moved Ndiga from unconfirmed to
   confirmed in `bataka.ts` (31/3/22 now, was 30/3/23); enriched the
   **Ngeye** note with the 2016 Kabaka-on-appeal resolution it was missing;
   enriched the **Mmamba** note with the 2025 Gabunga succession ruling
   detail. Flagged the Engeye/Kalindaluzzi 2026 case as a possibly-distinct
   spelling from Ngeye rather than silently linking them, since the source
   doesn't confirm they're the same clan.

4. **`/queens` merged into `/kabaka`.** Same pattern as the Bataka→Clans and
   Katikkiro/Amasaza→Abakungu merges from s10. All six Queens sections
   (Three Women/Three Crowns, Nnabagereka origins, current Nnabagereka
   bio/achievements, Namasole, Lubuga, Why Women Held Such Power) inserted
   into `KabakaContent.tsx` right after "Achievements Under Ssabasajja Kabaka
   Mutebi II" (which already teased the Nnabagereka partnership) and before
   "Symbols, Succession & Cultural Significance." Nothing condensed — full
   content carried over. Quick Reference table absorbed the Nnabagereka/
   Namasole/Lubuga rows in place of the old single "Queen consort" row.
   `QueensContent.tsx` deleted; `/queens/page.tsx` is now a `redirect
   ("/kabaka")` (same as `/bataka`'s pattern, so old bookmarks still land
   right). `/abalangira`'s companion-links grid no longer links to `/queens`
   separately — its `/kabaka` card now covers both.

5. **Nav tabs renamed and reordered.** New order: Home (Dashboard) → Ssabasajja
   Kabaka (was "Kabaka") → Abalangira n'Abambejja (was "Abalangira") → Ebika
   bya Baganda (was "Clans") → Abakungu ba Ssaabasajja (was "Abakungu") →
   Give → Profile. Sub-captions and all page content below the nav were left
   untouched, per explicit instruction. Used the codebase's own established
   spelling for the two Ssaabasajja-adjacent renames (single-*a*
   "Ssabasajja Kabaka" for the king's personal title, double-*a* "Abakungu ba
   Ssaabasajja" for the chiefs' network) rather than the user's shorthand
   typing, since both spellings already coexist in the source PDFs and the
   codebase had already picked one for each — flagged this choice to the user
   rather than silently deviating.
