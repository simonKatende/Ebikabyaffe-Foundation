## What was done this session (s10)

This was a long session covering several distinct pieces of work, roughly
in order:

1. **Bataka merged into the Clans tab.** `/bataka` no longer stands alone —
   `/clans` now has two views via `ClanViewTabs.tsx`: the Ebika grid
   (default) and a Bataka view (`?view=bataka`, title-led Mutaka cards with
   search + status filter chips). The old `/bataka` route now redirects
   there. Nav is one tab lighter; Clans' subtitle reads "Ebika & Bataka."

2. **Clan totem images.** Downloaded 45 real Wikimedia Commons photos (one
   per identifiable totem/akabbiro species) into `web/public/images/totems/`,
   with a registry (`web/lib/clanImages.ts`) carrying src/alt/credit per
   clan. Wired into the clan grid tiles and each clan detail page (hero +
   Omuziro/Akabbiro gallery). 11 clans with abstract totems (rainwater,
   heart, princely lines, unverified species) intentionally keep the emoji
   — don't force a photo onto those. Every human-adjacent photo was visually
   verified before use; two were swapped for cultural accuracy (Africanis
   village dog instead of Wikipedia's huskies for Mbwa; Termitomyces on an
   actual termite mound for Butiko).

3. **Tap-to-view lightbox.** Built `ImageLightbox.tsx` and wired it into
   every totem photo across the clan grid, clan detail pages, and the two
   hand-curated deep pages (Mmamba, Lugave) — tap any totem photo to see it
   full-screen with caption + credit.

4. **Footer layout fix.** The footer used to be `fixed` at the viewport
   bottom with a flat `88px` body padding to match — broke whenever the
   wrapping contact line grew past that guess and covered page content.
   Replaced with a flex "sticky footer" (`body` is `flex flex-col
   min-h-screen`, `<main>` is `flex-1`), so the footer sits in normal
   document flow and only looks pinned to the bottom on short pages. The
   Toast popup, which had been positioned `bottom-[88px]` to sit "above the
   footer," was repositioned to a plain `bottom-6` since that assumption no
   longer holds.

5. **Bataka current-holder names + photo research.** Names now show
   directly on the collapsed Mutaka card (not hidden behind a click).
   Did the requested photo research properly — and it came back empty: the
   Kingdom's own clan pages carry no personal photos; a promising-looking
   YouTube series ("Ttabamiruka w'Abataka 2022") turned out to be a
   *different* UK/Ireland diaspora event with its own representatives (zero
   name overlap — would have been a wrong-face risk); a news thumbnail of an
   actual 2024 installation was a multi-person group shot with no
   per-person caption. Along the way, found and fixed two stale
   current-holder entries (Mpologoma, Kiwere — both predecessors had died
   and been succeeded by their sons) and added dispute notes for Lugave and
   Nkejje. **Don't re-run this research** — it's already been done
   thoroughly; see `web/lib/bataka.ts` notes.

6. **`/abalangira` split out of `/kabaka`.** The royal clan (Olulyo
   Olulangira) got its own full page so the entire source PDF could be
   represented — birth/naming/succession mechanics, the full 10-row titles
   table, and the "clan rotation" theory had all been condensed out of the
   original `/kabaka` version. `/kabaka` now cross-links to it.

7. **`/katikkiro` removed, replaced by `/abakungu`.** User asked to fold the
   Katikkiro in as "head" of the "Abakungu ba Ssaabasajja" network. The
   first version of the source PDF didn't actually say that (it named a
   different person, Geoffrey Kibuuka, as head of the one documented UK
   sub-structure) — **paused and flagged this via `AskUserQuestion` before
   doing anything destructive**, rather than guessing or proceeding blindly.
   User supplied a corrected v2 PDF with an explicit new section confirming
   the Katikkiro *is* the administrative head, and work proceeded from
   there. New page leads with the full former Katikkiro profile, then the
   Ssaabasajja-honorific explainer, the hierarchy card, named chiefs in
   contested Buluuli/Kooki + elsewhere, and the UK diaspora roster (20
   officials). This pattern (verify a destructive-restructure premise
   against the actual source before executing) is saved as a memory —
   worth continuing for any future "remove/merge this page" request.

8. **`/amasaza` removed, merged into `/abakungu`.** Same session, follow-up
   request: since the Ssaza chiefs on `/abakungu` literally administer the
   Amasaza counties, merge them "where they best fit" rather than tack on.
   Extended the existing hierarchy card with the missing Kyalo/village tier
   instead of leaving two overlapping hierarchy explanations; slotted "What
   Are the Amasaza?" + "Origins & the Lost Counties" right before the
   Buluuli/Kooki section (Buluuli's rival claim *is* the same historical
   fault line, so it reads as one continuous story); embedded the reused
   `AmasazaGrid` component + Ssese + Masaza Cup directly above the
   named-chiefs table instead of just cross-linking. `AmasazaGrid.tsx` and
   the `amasaza`/`Ssaza` data in `clans.ts` were kept — only the standalone
   page+route were deleted. Nav is down to 8 tabs total now.

9. **Home page grew real content.** The logged-out landing page
   (`HomeLanding.tsx`) was a single 100vh hero with nothing below it — added
   About/Mission, a 6-person Leadership roster, a "Three Pillars of
   Buganda" section (each pillar links to its already-built page:
   `/abalangira`, `/clans?view=bataka`, `/abakungu` — doubles as a site
   map), the Wakivule flagship-school project, and a Recent Activity note
   (Jan 2025 SHIPU meeting) — all condensed from a real
   `Ebikabyaffe_Foundation.pdf` reference doc. **This is the first real
   organizational content in the app** (actual leadership names/roles,
   actual Kingdom affiliation) rather than mocked demo data like the rest of
   the app so far — treat it as load-bearing fact. Also used the same doc
   to fix two stale facts on already-built pages: `/abalangira`'s
   Ssabalangira mention now names Godfrey Kikulwe Musanje (died 3 Feb 2026)
   and interim successor Prince Frederick Sunday Kateregga; `/abakungu`'s
   Katikkiro quick-ref now notes "13 years in post as of May 2026."

10. **YouTube video embeds.** Built a reusable `YouTubeEmbed.tsx` (thumbnail
    + play button → swaps to a `youtube-nocookie.com` iframe with
    `autoplay=1` only once tapped — nothing loads or plays until then).
    Wired the real Foundation launch video (`A9fa60aNhQA`) into the About
    section and the Wakivule school launch video (`3CEUzFGR3Fo`) into the
    Flagship Project section. Verified both thumbnails resolve to real,
    matching BBS TV news footage before wiring them in.

11. **Footer motto + hero cleanup.** Changed the global site footer's motto
    from the placeholder "Agali awamu — Enkulaakulana..." to the
    Foundation's actual stated objective, "Okutumbula n'okusitula
    Ebikabyaffe — To promote and uplift our heritage" (source: the same
    Foundation PDF). Removed a duplicated trust-bar strip (NGO reg number,
    Kabaka name, bilingual badge, and eventually the leftover motto line
    too) from the hero. Along the way, caught and fixed a real layout bug:
    the hero's vertically-centered content stack had no reserved bottom
    space, so on typical viewport heights the stats bar crowded down into
    the same zone where the slider prints its place-name captions — fixed
    with a `paddingBottom: 110px` reserve on the hero section rather than a
    cosmetic nudge, so it holds regardless of viewport height.
