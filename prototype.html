Origin Story

The Mmamba clan is one of the oldest and most distinguished of the 52 Buganda clans. According to oral tradition, the Mmamba trace their origins to Kintu, the first Kabaka of Buganda, through the lineage of Nambi. The lungfish — which can survive both in water and on land, and endure long dry seasons buried in the earth — became the clan's totem as a symbol of resilience, adaptability, and enduring life.

The Mmamba are historically associated with the shores of Lake Victoria and the Sese Islands, where the lungfish is found in abundance. Many notable Baganda throughout history — including chiefs, warriors, and scholars — have been of the Mmamba clan.

Sigas (Sub-clan branches)

  • Nkima

    The Monkey siga — associated with the central Buganda region and historically connected to the royal court.

  • Kasimba

    The Genet Cat siga — found predominantly in Masaka and Rakai districts.

  • Nkerebwe

    The Crocodile siga — historically the siga of warriors; associated with the Sese Islands and the lake shore.

Omutaka of the Mmamba Clan · Serving since 2008

Ssabataka Ndiwalana has led the Mmamba clan for over 16 years, presiding over annual clan gatherings at Munyonyo and representing Mmamba interests in the Buganda Council of Clan Heads. He oversees member verification and clan governance.

Taboos (Mizizo)

  • A Mmamba may not eat the lungfish (omuziro)
  • A Mmamba may not eat sheep or mutton (akabbiro)
  • A Mmamba may not kill or mistreat a lungfish; if one is found on land it must be returned to water
  • A Mmamba may not marry another Mmamba — exogamy is strictly observed
